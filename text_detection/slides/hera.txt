Hera, the queen of the gods and wife of Zeus, is known as the goddess of marriage and family. Often portrayed as regal and majestic, she embodies the ideal of womanhood but is also characterized by her jealousy and vengeful nature towards Zeus's many lovers and offspring. Despite her tumultuous relationship with Zeus, Hera plays a crucial role in many myths as a protector of women during childbirth and marriage. She was worshipped throughout Greece with grand temples dedicated to her majesty, including the famous Heraion at Olympia. Her sacred animals include peacocks and cows, symbolizing beauty and fertility respectively. Hera's complex character reflects both the strength and vulnerability inherent in marital relationships.
